Introduction

The Euros 2020 were supposed to be played between 12 June - 12 July for the first time across several host nations to commemorate its' 60th anniversary. It would have been a summer full of memorable goals, underdog stories, last-minute winners and a tournament that would be remembered for many years after its' completion.

However, due to the global Covid-19 pandemic, it's rather a summer confined to the four-walls of our own homes, in which we are forced to watch replays of Greece's unlikely success in 2004, Spain's tiki-taka dominance inbetween 2008 and 2012, and England's disappointing exit against Iceland in 2016, as UEFA Euro 2020 was officially rescheduled to 11 June - 11 July 2021.

Sweden v Germany (0W-2D-6L, St Petersburg)

Last Match: 1-2 (2018)

“When they are down, when they are tested, they find a win!”, who does not remember Toni Kroos’ freekick from the narrowest of angles in the 90+5 minute to clinch a 2-1 victory for the Germans against Sweden in the 2018 World Cup. It was such a high-paced game, Germany was pressing extremely high-up the pitch and left themselves vulnerable on the break, which the Swedes used to their advantage.

Although both teams have unarguably only improved in these two years, their playing philosophy has stayed the exact same, high-pressing possession against defensive transition football. However, the key difference to this rematch is that Germany has since then adopted the 3-4-3 formation in contrast to their traditional 4-2-3-1.

With the 3-4-3 formation, Germany is much harder to break down and highly adaptable to any opponent (©Bundesliga.)

The new 3 at the back formation would make it much more difficult for the Swedes to counter against and could potential lead to a comfortable victory for the Germans. We expect the Germans to have around 70% ball possession (average of the last 3 games between both sides) and be much more defensively stable compared to 2018.

QF1 Result: Germany 2-0 Sweden

Denmark v Italy (3W-2D-8L, Munich)

Last Match: 2-2 (2013)

Italy’s head-to-head record and squad strength make them the clear favourites for this tie, however, what makes knockout competitions so exciting is that underdogs often upset the odds. We just have to look as far as to Wales back in 2016 (or Russia in 2008 and Greece in 2004 for that matter) to see that it is more than common to see underdog success stories during the Euros. Wales made it into the semi-finals past an extremely strong Belgium side, in a match that was absolutely no fluke, but rather a world-class display of the whole Welsh national team.

Talent and team spirit all over the pitch sends Denmark through to the semi-finals (©salajean / Shutterstock.com).

This is why we predict that this 2020's Wales comes in the form of Denmark ,with a few quality players and a strong believe, they manage to dominate a game against the Italians. Eriksen, Poulsen and Bruun Larsen are at the heart of Denmark’s astonishing performance against Italy. Even though most of the predictions until now are built on statistics, there is nothing more satisfying to see in the world of football than David beating Goliath.

Last Match: 1-1 (2018)

Belgium’s golden generation against the Netherland’s upcoming golden generation, what an exciting match-up this is. Ever since Roberto Martinez took over the Belgium national team after UEFA Euro 2016 and incorporated the 3-4-3 formation, the team has been playing extraordinarily well, even topping the FIFA world rankings. After a third-place finish at the 2018 World Cup, the team will be extra motivated as this could be the last time that this generation of players (including Hazard, Mertens, De Bruyne, Vertonghen etc.) are able to challenge for a major title.

Despite their world-class starting eleven, which could possibly even be the best in the world, Belgium’s weakness lies in their squad depth. Playing a 3-4-3 formation requires high amounts of energy and tactical understanding of all the players in the team. However, unlike other teams like Germany, Belgium is lacking options to replace the stars in the starting eleven and we predict that this could lead to Belgians’ demise. Add to this Koeman’s Netherlands with their classical Dutch 4-3-3 total-football philosophy, it is going to be a very tough match. If two or three of the Belgian stars miss out on this game due to bookings or injuries, we believe that the game could pen in favour of the Dutch.

Three words to sum-up this young Dutch side: Confidence, Creativity and Control (©Piotr Piatrouski / Shutterstock.com).

What makes the Netherlands extremely dangerous for Belgium is their adaptability to different situations, as the team can transition very fast and attack out wide via Promes and Bergwijn or keep the ball and play through the lines with a very technical backline (De ligt, Van Dijk, Blind) and midfield (De Jong, Wijnaldum, De Roon/Van De Beek). We predict that someone from the Belgium midfield might miss this match and let the Netherlands take control in the midfield, which ultimately decides the game, although there is not a lot that separate both sides.

QF3 Result: Netherlands 3-1 Belgium (FT 1-1)

Switzerland v England (3W-6D-16L, Rome)

Last Match: 0-0, 5-6 PEN (2019)

Less than a year ago, when England faced Switzerland in the UEFA Nations League match for third place, it felt like the goal posts had a spell over them. Although England dominated the whole game (60% possession and 5 shots on target compared to Switzerland’s 2), they left it to the penalty shootout to grab the win against Switzerland.

This time around, the Three Lions are a much more complete team as mentioned before and Sancho, who has been a relevation so far in the tournament is once again a key figure for the English side. With trickery in his feet, an eye for a pass and goal, the Swiss like find it hard to cope with the young Borussia Dortmund winger.

England's talisman Jadon Sancho has been the best player in the tournament so far (©Vlad 1988 / Shutterstock.com).

As favourites to win the tournament and extra motivation to play the final two rounds at home, we predict that the Three Lions will not face too many issues against Switzerland. Compared to Spain, who lost to Switzerland because of bad luck and lack of physicality, England’s squad is in incredibly scoring form and match the physicality of any side in the world and should win comfortably against the Swiss, who have not won against England since 1980.

QF4 Result: Switzerland 0-3 England

Germany v Denmark (3W-2D-3L, London)

Last Match: 1-1 (2017)

No one could have expected such an equal head-to-head record between these two sides, as most teams will have a negative record against the Germans. Surprisingly, the last game between them ended in a 1-1 draw in 2017, however, it was a friendly in which Germany did experiment quite a bit with their starting eleven.

Statistically, Germany is the better side than Denmark, averaging more possession, shots, passes, xG and much more on average, but the Danish are one of the toughest sides to beat in International Football, having not lost a single competitive game since missing out on Euro 2016, except two games in the 2018 World Cup, this team’s strength lies in its stability and lack of mistakes.

Injury-plagued Marco Reus enjoys his first proper international tournament and leads Germany's young offensive line (©Michele Morrone / Shutterstock.com).

However, even considering all these facts, we predict that Germany will still come out victorious in this semi-final match. The Danish do have the quality to surprise any opposition in the tournament, as seen in their 3-1 against Italy, and we predict that they could make it very difficult for the Germans. In the end, the game could see some goals as this match-up promises 2.5 goals on average. After a tough nerve-wrecking game it could be decided via a penalty shoot-out, in which Germany’s elite mentality sees them through into the final.

England v Netherlands (6W-9D-7L, London)

Last Match: 1-3 (2019)

England and the Netherlands meet once again in a semi-final. Just a year prior these two faced each other in a thrilling game in the semi-finals of the UEFA Nations League, a game that is remembered by its’ multiple individual mistakes by defenders that led to 3 of the 4 goals and a famous 3-1 win for the Dutch. However, this time around, the audience can expect a much more composed defence on either side.

Having said that, the starting line-up of both sides will likely to be very similar to the one in the Nations League, with the only exceptions probably being Alexander-Arnold and Henderson replacing Walker and Rice/Delph. As a result, we predict that this match will develop similarly to the previous game. The last match, despite the highlights, was almost completely dominated by the Dutch (53% possession, 28 shots and 12 shots on target), with England having difficulties (47% possession, 15 shots and 3 shots on target) to establish any attacking rhythm.

At the heart of all of this was Frenkie De Jong. The current Barcelona midfielder is regarded as a generational talent and even though he does not offer much threat in the attacking third of the pitch, remains incredibly calm under pressure and always finds the right positive pass to lead his team up the pitch from his defensive midfield position.

Midfield Maestro Fenkie De Jong dictates the tempo of the whole game against England (©Vlad1988 / Shutterstock.com).

What highlighted the previous match was a frustration on England’s side of losing control in midfield and running after the ball for majority of the game. Therefore, despite England’s world-class attacking threat, which brought the team past Switzerland and World Cup winners France, we predict that the Dutch will once again dominate the midfield and prevent the Three Lions' attacking talent from shining.

SF2 Result: England 1-2 Netherlands
